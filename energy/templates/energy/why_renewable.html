Why Renewable Energy Matters

Together, we can create a sustainable future—before it's too late.

Why We Need to Act Now

The global reliance on non-renewable resources is not just unsustainable—it's catastrophic. In 2023, fossil fuels still made up 84% of global energy consumption (Our World in Data). This addiction to oil, coal, and gas is depleting our planet's natural resources and pushing us toward irreversible damage. We are running out of time.

Environmental Impact

According to the IPCC's Sixth Assessment Report, if global temperatures rise more than 1.5°C, we will see extreme climate conditions—more floods, droughts, wildfires, and loss of biodiversity. Transitioning to renewable energy can reduce global CO2 emissions by up to 70% by 2050.

Economic Benefits

Studies by the International Renewable Energy Agency (IRENA) suggest that switching to renewables could boost global GDP by $98 trillion by 2050, while creating millions of jobs in green industries.

Energy Security

With fossil fuels running out and energy demands increasing, countries are facing serious energy security issues. The 2021 Texas power crisis (New York Times) highlighted the vulnerabilities in relying on outdated, centralized power grids fueled by coal and gas. Renewable energy is decentralized, sustainable, and available everywhere.

Personal Responsibility

Did you know that the average American household is responsible for 50 metric tons of CO2 emissions annually? (NY Times: Reduce Your Carbon Footprint) By adopting solar power, reducing energy waste, and supporting green initiatives, we can all contribute to global sustainability.
